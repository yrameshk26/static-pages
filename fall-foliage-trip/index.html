👶 Family & Stroller Guide

🚫 Where to leave the stroller

  • Flume Gorge: Absolutely requires a baby carrier/backpack. 2 miles of stairs, steep inclines, and boardwalks.
  • Scenic Trains: Aisles are too narrow. Strollers must be folded tight or left safely at the station platform (very common).
  • Waterfalls (Kancamagus): Short dirt/rocky paths to the falls require walking or carrying.

✅ Where strollers work great

  • North Conway Village: Great sidewalks for strolling, shopping, and grabbing coffee.
  • Scenic Pull-offs: The parking areas on the Kancamagus Highway are paved and offer great views right from the car.
  • Lake Placid (Mirror Lake): Paved path around the lake if you stop to stretch your legs on Day 2.

Scenic Train Options

New Hampshire · Book Early

Conway Scenic Railroad Booked

North Conway, NH

Departs from a beautiful 1874 Victorian station. The Mountaineer route is a 4-5 hour journey traversing the stunning Crawford Notch. It's widely considered the most classic "fall foliage" train.

Mount Washington Cog Railway

Bretton Woods, NH

A thrilling, historic bio-diesel or steam train that climbs straight up the side of Mount Washington (the highest peak in the Northeast). Spectacular, sweeping views above the tree line.

Granite State Railway (Fall Foliage Train)

Lincoln, NH / Meredith, NH

A very relaxing, family-friendly ride that hugs the banks of the Pemigewasset River or Lake Winnipesaukee, getting you right up close to the vibrant changing leaves.

Cafe Lafayette Dinner Train

North Woodstock, NH

If you want to combine an activity with dinner, this 1950s restored vintage rail car (with a dome!) serves an elegant 5-course meal while rolling 20 miles through the river valley at sunset.
